# Price Research – Batch 4 (Items 31–40)

Compiled 2026-04-22. Primary sources: Noble Knight Games (CA storefront, prices in CAD;
~CA$1.00 = ~US$0.72 at time of research), eBay active and "for sale online" listing snapshots,
publisher sites and AbeBooks references. Direct eBay sold-listing pages were largely
inaccessible during this research session (timeouts), so several "sold" ranges below are
inferred from current asking prices on Noble Knight, eBay active listings and recent listing
snapshots (i.e., realistic-asking ranges, not necessarily strict completed-sale data). Where
that's the case it is flagged as "asking-price only" in the Notes section.

USD values from CAD listings are computed at CA$1 ≈ US$0.72.

## 31. Laws of the Wild — White Wolf / Mind's Eye Theatre
**Year/edition:** Werewolf: The Apocalypse Mind's Eye Theatre LARP rulebook. The original
1997 release (Thomas Stratman) was sometimes called the "2nd" Apocalypse-style edition for
MET; later WW reprints/revisions exist (2001 revised by Kravit/Baugh, ISBN 9781588465016).
**ISBN/stock:** 1997 edition ISBN 1565045084 / 9781565045088 (often catalogued as WW5102 in
collector catalogs).
**Recent sold-listing range:** ~$10–$25 USD (asking-price only)
**Suggested asking price (used-good):** ~$15 USD
**Notes:** This is one of the most common MET supplements. Multiple active eBay listings
exist (combined-shipping copies often around $10–$15). The 2001 revised hardcover ("Laws of
the Wild – Revised Rules for Playing Werewolf", ISBN 9781588465016) trades a little higher
than the 1997 trade paperback but is also widely available. Condition matters less than for
rarer titles; the WoD MET LARP line is a slow market with lots of supply.
**Examples:**
- Active 2026 — eBay UK, "Fair+" 1st-edition listing visible at low single-digit GBP
  before shipping ([eBay 156513451634](https://www.ebay.com/itm/156513451634))
- Active 2026 — multiple combined-shipping 1997 edition listings around $10–$15
  ([eBay 256221639884](https://www.ebay.com/itm/256221639884))

## 32. The Shining Host — White Wolf / Mind's Eye Theatre
**Year/edition:** Changeling: The Dreaming for Mind's Eye Theatre, by Peter Woodworth.
Published **1998** (the user-supplied "1997" date appears to be off by one year per Amazon
and Goodreads metadata). Softcover, 232 pages.
**ISBN/stock:** ISBN 1565045106 / 9781565045101. Catalog code is **WW5009** (not WW5200 —
WW5200 is the MET Vampire/Masquerade 2nd Ed catalog; the user's source likely transposed
the code).
**Recent sold-listing range:** ~$8–$25 USD
**Suggested asking price (used-good):** ~$15 USD
**Notes:** Noble Knight CA currently lists a VG copy at CA$8.70 (~US$6.30), heavily
discounted from MSRP CA$21.76. eBay active listings cluster in the low- to mid-teens. As
with Laws of the Wild, this is a common MET supplement and not particularly scarce; price is
condition-driven. The hardcover versions of MET books are scarcer than the paperbacks but
this title appears to be SC primarily. If the copy is the Player's Guide variant rather than
the main rulebook, expect similar pricing.
**Examples:**
- Active 2026 — Noble Knight CA, VG, CA$8.70 (~US$6.30)
  ([NK listing](https://ca.nobleknight.com/P/9053/Shining-Host-The))
- Active 2026 — eBay listing for Shining Host SC ($15-ish range)
  ([eBay 195565552525](https://www.ebay.com/itm/195565552525))

## 33. Legend of the Five Rings RPG (1st Edition) — AEG
**Year/edition:** 1st edition, 1997, John Wick, AEG 3001, hardcover 256pp.
**ISBN/stock:** ISBN 1-887953-30-8 / 9781887953009.
**Recent sold-listing range:** ~$30–$70 USD depending on condition
**Suggested asking price (used-good):** ~$40–$50 USD
**Notes:** A core 1990s RPG with steady collector demand. The book is famous as the seed of
the L5R CCG/RPG empire and the Rokugan setting. Original hardcover holds value better than
many 90s RPGs because of strong nostalgia + reprint scarcity (later reprints exist but the
1st printing is desirable). Foil/limited-cover variants of subsequent printings sell at
premiums. Damaged copies (loose binding, water damage) drop fast — condition really
matters here.
**Examples:**
- Listed 2026 — Dragon's Trove, Near Mint, **$35.00 USD**
  ([Dragon's Trove](https://www.dragonstrove.com/products/legend-of-the-five-rings-1st-edition))
- Listed 2026 — RPG Reliquary, Very Good, **$55.00 USD** (sold out, indicating recent demand)
  ([RPG Reliquary](https://rpgreliquary.com/en-us/products/legend-of-the-five-rings-core-rulebook-aeg3001))
- Active eBay 2026 — multiple listings in the $35–$70 range depending on edition/printing
  ([eBay 156357521013](https://www.ebay.com/itm/156357521013))

## 37. In Nomine (Limited Edition, White / "Angelic") — Steve Jackson Games
**Year/edition:** 1997, hardcover 208pp, leatherette-bound limited reprint of the first
printing. The Angelic (white) and Demonic (black) leatherette editions were both produced;
the White is somewhat scarcer than the Black on the secondary market.
**ISBN/stock:** SJG3304-W (white) / SJG3304-B (black). Standard 1997 hardcover/softcover is
SJG3301 / ISBN 9781556342813.
**Recent sold-listing range:** Limited White: ~$45–$75 USD. Standard 1st printing softcover:
~$15–$25 USD.
**Suggested asking price (used-good):** **~$50 USD for the Limited White (VG/EX)**;
add ~10–15% for VG/NM, drop ~15–20% if shelf-worn or with bumped corners.
**Notes:** There **is** a clear price premium for the limited leatherette editions over the
standard hardcover — roughly 2–3× the price of a comparable-condition standard hardcover.
Noble Knight CA stocks White Limited at CA$65–70 (~US$47–50). Black Limited consistently
runs slightly higher on Noble Knight (CA$113–145 / ~US$81–104), suggesting Black > White in
collector demand at the moment, though both are clearly scarcer than the standard release.
The standard 1997 1st-printing softcover trades at roughly CA$13–20 (~US$10–15) at Noble
Knight, confirming a strong premium for the leatherette variants.
**Examples:**

## 38. Codex: Tyranids (2nd Edition) — Games Workshop
**Year/edition:** 1995, Warhammer 40K 2nd Edition, 87pp softcover.
**ISBN/stock:** Games Workshop, no commercial ISBN typical for 2nd-ed codices.
**Recent sold-listing range:** ~$60–$130 USD for clean copies; rough copies $30–$60
**Suggested asking price (used-good):** ~$60–$80 USD as-is given disclosed wear and
loose binding; clean VG copies command $100+
**Notes:** Highly sought after as one of the rarer 2nd-edition 40K codices — Tyranids 2nd
ed is a fan favourite (the era of Hive Tyrants on Carnifex bases, the "godzilla" army).
Noble Knight CA currently asks **CA$181/174 (~US$130/125)** for VG+/VG copies, which is at
the top of the market. eBay active listings show wider spread, including $94.99 international
listings. The user's copy has acknowledged wear and loose binding — that drops it materially:
expect $50–$80 in the buyer's mind even though "good" copies trade higher. Spine
integrity, completeness of the colour-plate inserts, and absence of writing/highlighting are
the biggest price drivers.
**Examples:**

## 40. Herbalist's Primer Bundle — Exalted Funeral / Anna Urbanek
**Year/edition:** 2022 main book; bundle is the publisher's standing offer.
**ISBN/stock:** Main book ISBN 978-83-67889-49-1 (also catalogued under 9789526989419
for an earlier printing).
**Recent sold-listing range:** ~$50–$80 USD as a complete set; main hardcover alone
$30–$50 USD on the secondary market
**Suggested asking price (used-good):** **~$50–$60 USD for the complete bundle**
(matches publisher's current sale price), or pricing components separately:
hardcover ~$35, notebook ~$10, zine ~$8, poster ~$5
**Notes:** Important context — **the publisher (Exalted Funeral) currently sells the
complete bundle new for $50** (sale, regular $100). That sets a hard ceiling on
secondary-market pricing: a used bundle should be priced *below* $50 to move quickly,
unless the listing makes a point that the buyer can avoid international shipping, etc.
The user's bundle composition ("Primer + Song of the Hedgerow + Flash Cards") is slightly
different from Exalted Funeral's current "Primer + Notebook + Adventure Zine + Poster"
bundle, so the components matter when comparing. The Flash Cards are a Kickstarter add-on
not in the current retail bundle, so they may add a small premium for completionist
buyers. The book is well-regarded in OSR/indie circles (won/featured in several lists);
demand is healthy but supply is plentiful since it was Kickstarted at scale and remains
in print. Hardcover used copies on eBay/Pangobooks generally land $30–$50.
**Examples:**
- Live 2026 — Exalted Funeral, **NEW bundle on sale at US$50** (regular $100) — this is

## Cross-cutting notes

- **eBay sold-listing pages were inaccessible** during this research session (60s timeouts
  on every `LH_Sold=1` URL and most individual item URLs). Where possible I substituted
  Noble Knight Games (which sells used-graded inventory and is a reasonable proxy for
  asking-price ceiling), eBay active listings, and other secondary-market vendors.
- **Noble Knight Canada vs US**: The .com URLs increasingly redirect to ca.nobleknight.com,
  and prices there are listed in CAD. Apply ~0.72× to convert to USD.
- **WW catalog code correction**: The user's source listed The Shining Host as **WW5200**
  but the canonical WoD/MET catalog code is **WW5009**. WW5200 maps to MET Vampire:
  The Masquerade 2nd Edition. This was likely a transposition in the source materials.
- **Year correction**: The Shining Host appears to have a 1998 (not 1997) publication date
  per multiple bibliographic sources.
- **Condition baseline**: All "used-good" suggested prices assume complete book, no
  writing/highlighting, no major water damage, intact binding, and only light shelf wear.
  Reduce 25–40% for materially worse copies (loose pages, missing inserts, ex-library, etc.).
