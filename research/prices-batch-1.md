# Price Research — Batch 1 (10 products)

Research date: 2026-04-22
Currency: USD unless otherwise noted
Sources: PicClick (eBay aggregator), Noble Knight Games, Miniature Market, The Game Steward, BoardGameGeek/RPGGeek, Biblio, Worthpoint, plus general web search.

Note: Direct fetches of eBay sold-listing pages timed out repeatedly during this research. PicClick (which surfaces eBay listings and sold data) and Noble Knight Games (a major secondhand game retailer that grades by condition) were used as primary substitutes. Where listings are "current" rather than "sold," that is noted; current asking prices in a thin market are still a reasonable proxy for what a copy will move at.

## 1. Chaos in the Old World — Fantasy Flight Games / Games Workshop
**Year/edition:** 2009, 1st edition (1st or 2nd printing). Out of print since FFG lost the Warhammer license. (Anniversary Edition 2024 is a separate product and trades much cheaper.)
**ISBN/stock:** 9781589946514
**Recent sold-listing range:** $100–$320 USD (used to sealed). Most used/complete copies cluster at $150–$260.
**Suggested asking price (used-good, complete):** $180–$220 USD
**Notes:**
- Strong collector demand. The Eric Lang Warhammer game is widely considered a modern classic and has been OOP for years.
- Condition matters a lot: a sealed/shrinkwrapped 1st printing routinely fetches $250–$320; played-but-complete copies sit in the $150–$220 range.
- The included Horned Rat expansion (separate product) bundled in with the base raises value substantially.
- Anniversary Edition (2024) is cheaper and more available — make sure your listing clearly states "1st edition / 1st printing / OOP" to capture the premium.
- Noble Knight Canada is currently OOS on the 2nd-printing copy (last stocked 4/20/2026), suggesting demand outpaces supply.
**Examples:**
- 1st printing, NM condition — sold $109 + $19 shipping (PicClick / eBay listing, ~15 days for sale)
- Painted 1st edition (UK) — sold £107 (~$135 USD)
- Various current asks: $239.95 (played once), $260 (VG/EX box), $300 (open box), $310–$320 (new sealed 1st ed)
- 1st edition box, Fair/EX condition — listed for sale on eBay (item 156669472391)

## 2. Arkham Horror (2nd Edition) — Fantasy Flight Games
**Year/edition:** 2005, 2nd edition base game (2nd printing; OOP)
**ISBN/stock:** FFG product code VA01
**Recent sold-listing range:** $25–$90 USD for complete used copies; sealed/shrink copies $85–$110
**Suggested asking price (used-good, complete):** $50–$65 USD
**Notes:**
- Was the flagship FFG Cthulhu game for over a decade. Replaced by Arkham Horror 3rd Edition (2018), which softened demand for the 2nd ed somewhat.
- Big board, lots of small components — completeness is critical. "100% complete" listings command a clear premium over "missing 1 card / 1 token" copies.
- Without the many expansions, base game alone tops out around $80 used. Lots that include expansions (Dunwich Horror, Kingsport, etc.) routinely sell in the $300–$500 range.
- Noble Knight Canada has multiple grades in stock (~CA$58–CA$87 = ~US$42–$63), confirming a healthy mid-double-digit market.
**Examples:**
- "100% Complete Revised Printing" — $49.99 BIN (PicClick/eBay, current)
- Base game, used, complete — $30.00 BIN-or-best-offer (PicClick/eBay)
- Brand new in shrink — $87.82 BIN
- Noble Knight (CA): VG+/NM, unpunched — CA$87.02 (~US$63); Fair/NM with rules VG+ — CA$69.62 (~US$51)

## 4. Pathfinder Core Rulebook (2e, pre-Remaster) — Paizo
**Year/edition:** 2019 hardcover (640pp), pre-Remaster. ISBN 978-1-64078-559-5 (alt ISBN 9781640781689 also seen).
**Recent sold-listing range:** $20–$55 USD for the standard hardcover; deluxe/leather editions $65–$140+
**Suggested asking price (used-good):** $25–$32 USD
**Notes:**
- The 2023 Remaster (Player Core / GM Core) explicitly replaced this book. Paizo will not reprint it. New copies are still floating around at clearance prices, which keeps the secondhand floor low.
- Demand has dropped meaningfully since the Remaster: most buyers want the Remaster instead. A few collectors/players still want the original (it has all the OGL-derived content the Remaster scrubbed).
- Standard hardcover seldom clears $40 unless near-mint or sealed. Deluxe (leatherette / pocket / special editions) hold value much better.
- Realistic recommendation: price aggressively if you want it to move — a used-good standard hardcover at $30 will likely sell; at $50 it may sit.
**Examples:**
- Standard hardcover, BIN — $23.75 (PicClick/eBay)
- NM standard hardcover — $25–$26 BIN-or-best-offer
- "Very Good w/ cosmetic wear" — $32.99 BOO
- Excellent condition standard hardcover — $34.99 BOO
- New copy — $53.95 BIN

**Recent sold-listing range:** $30–$47 USD for complete used/open copies; $40–$50 sealed

**Recent sold-listing range:** $30–$68 USD (used hardcover)

## Methodology & caveats

- **Direct eBay sold-listing pages timed out** during this research session (multiple attempts). PicClick aggregates eBay sold/active listings and was used as the primary substitute; Noble Knight Games (a major specialist secondhand RPG retailer with a published condition-grading system) provided cross-reference points.
- Several "sold" prices listed above are technically PicClick "current/recently active" listings rather than confirmed completed sales — the distinction is noted where relevant. In a thin market like OOP RPG/board games, current asks are a reasonable approximation.
- All USD conversions of CAD prices use approximate rate ~0.73 USD/CAD.
- For a clean median you can verify, the recommendation is to spot-check 2–3 of these on eBay's actual "Sold" filter before publishing prices on the sale site.
